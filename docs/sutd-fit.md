# Fit with SUTD MSc in Design and Artificial Intelligence for Enterprise

## Design

Super Brain 2.0 is a design-led system project. It begins with a human problem: knowledge becomes fragmented when projects, AI conversations, decisions, and execution records grow across many tools.

The project frames this problem as a workflow and information architecture challenge, not only a technical implementation challenge.

## Artificial Intelligence

AI is positioned as an assistant for structuring, retrieving, summarizing, and reasoning over project knowledge.

The project does not frame AI as a fully autonomous decision-maker. Instead, it explores how LLM-enabled workflows can support human judgment when paired with structure, governance, and review.

## Enterprise

Although the current version is personal and portfolio-safe, the underlying problem is highly relevant to enterprise settings.

Organizations also struggle with:

- Lost decisions
- Scattered knowledge across tools
- Poor source traceability
- Repeated work
- Weak handoff between teams
- Inconsistent AI usage

Super Brain 2.0 explores how an individual knowledge system can become a prototype for enterprise knowledge management patterns.

## Human-AI Interaction

The project emphasizes human-AI collaboration. AI helps organize and reason, while the human remains responsible for judgment, approval, and sensitive-data boundaries.

This matches my interest in how people understand, trust, and use AI systems in real workflows.

## Responsible AI and Knowledge Governance

The project includes explicit governance principles:

- Public/private boundary
- Sensitive data exclusion
- Human review
- Source traceability
- Decision versioning
- Write-back discipline

These principles are essential for responsible AI-assisted knowledge work.
